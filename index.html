Recognizing Allergy Symptoms

Does this sound familiar?

Allergy and asthma symptoms are often dismissed as a cold, a bad week, or seasonal annoyances. But when they're consistent, they have a cause — and a treatment.

Our Waco patients often tell us they wish they'd come in sooner. If any of these patterns match your experience, you don't have to keep managing through it alone.

Request an Evaluation
01

Nasal Congestion & Stuffy Nose

Persistent stuffiness that doesn't clear, especially indoors or in the morning — often a sign of environmental allergen exposure, not infection.

02

Sneezing & Runny Nose

Repeated sneezing fits, watery nasal discharge, or an itchy nose triggered by pollen, dust, pets, or mold — classic allergic rhinitis.

03

Itchy, Watery Eyes

Eye irritation, redness, or swelling that worsens outdoors or during peak pollen seasons is almost always allergic conjunctivitis — highly treatable.

04

Asthma & Wheezing

Chest tightness, shortness of breath, or wheezing — especially during exercise or allergy season — may indicate allergy-triggered or seasonal asthma.

05

Sinus Pressure & Headaches

Chronic facial pressure, sinus pain, or frequent headaches can result from untreated allergic inflammation rather than a structural sinus problem.

06

Skin Reactions & Hives

Eczema flare-ups, chronic hives, or contact dermatitis may reflect underlying allergies to foods, environmental triggers, or materials.

07

Food Reactions

Digestive distress, hives, or swelling after eating — food allergy evaluation provides clarity on which foods are safe and which require avoidance.

08

Chronic Cough & Throat Clearing

A cough that never fully resolves, or constant need to clear your throat, often traces back to post-nasal drip from uncontrolled allergies.

Immunotherapy — The Standard of Care

Allergy shots don't manage allergies.
They resolve them.

Most allergy medications suppress your immune response temporarily. When you stop taking them, symptoms return. Immunotherapy works differently: it gradually retrains your immune system so it no longer overreacts to allergens in the first place. The result is lasting relief — not just while you're in treatment, but for years afterward.

It's the closest thing to a cure that modern allergy medicine offers.

Treatment Timeline

A three-phase approach built around your life.

1

Phase 1 — Months 1–7

Build-Up Phase

Weekly injections with gradually increasing doses of your specific allergens. Your immune system begins adapting. Most patients notice a reduction in symptoms within the first few months.

2

Phase 2 — Months 8–36

Maintenance Phase

Injections shift to monthly visits at a consistent therapeutic dose. Symptom relief deepens. Many patients reduce or eliminate daily medications.

3

Phase 3 — Year 4–5

Long-Term Benefit

After completing the full course, most patients maintain significant relief for years — even after stopping injections. We monitor and support your long-term outcomes.

Why It Matters

There is a difference between managing allergies and treating them.

Antihistamines and nasal sprays work — but only while you take them. They address the immune response after it happens. Immunotherapy addresses why it happens. That distinction changes everything about the quality of your daily life.

Common Questions

Questions about allergy care in Waco.

Full Education Center
What is immunotherapy and how does it work? +

Immunotherapy (allergy shots) gradually desensitizes your immune system to specific allergens by introducing small, increasing doses over time. Over 3–5 years, most patients experience a significant reduction in symptoms — and many achieve long-lasting relief even after treatment ends. It's considered the most effective long-term treatment for environmental allergies.

When is pollen season in Waco, Texas? +

Waco has year-round pollen exposure with four distinct peaks: Mountain cedar (December–February) is the most intense. Oak and elm follow in spring (March–May). Grasses dominate summer (May–August). Ragweed and fall allergens peak September–November. Our clinic tracks local pollen data and can advise on timing your treatment accordingly.

Do you accept new patients? +

Yes. We welcome new patients at our Waco clinic. You can request an appointment online or call us directly. Most insurance plans are accepted, and our team can help verify your coverage before your first visit. No referral is required in most cases.

Does my child need a referral to see an allergist? +

In most cases, no referral is needed. We treat both children and adults. If your insurance plan requires a referral, your primary care physician can provide one, and our office team is happy to help coordinate.

How long does allergy testing take? +

A standard allergy skin test appointment takes approximately 60–90 minutes. Results are read at the clinic during your visit, so you'll leave with a clear picture of your sensitivities and a discussion of next steps on the same day.

Can allergies cause asthma? +

Yes. Allergic asthma is one of the most common forms of asthma, particularly in children. Environmental allergens like pollen, dust mites, pet dander, and mold can trigger inflammation in the airways that leads to asthma symptoms. Treating the underlying allergy through immunotherapy often significantly improves asthma control.
